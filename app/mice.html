M.I.C.E - heritage

×

The true charm of Goa lies within its cultural heritage. The Architecture in Goa is one of the remaining accounts of the wide spectrum of its rich heritage. The Architecture retains the functional elements of the pre-colonial period but also showcases the subsequent influence of European architecture with its elements of classical, gothic and manueline design forms. A combination of Indian and Portuguese architecture in the form of churches, temples, cathedrals, heritage houses and it grand forts, these places have a special cultural and physical significance. Moreover, the churches and convents in Old Goa are a UNESCO Wold heritage site.

With the legends and stories associated to all these places, a MICE event at these places is simply one of a kind. We have conceptualized various corporate events that can be executed in the midst of these historic monuments.

Alternately many of the resorts and hotels in Goa have drawn inspiration from this heritage which is evidently seen in their design and architecture. A setup at such locations will also rightly soak in the elements of heritage and bring a different dimension to the whole event.

Take advantage of this opportunity to reminiscence and celebrate your event at any of these glorious structures.

Beaches

Agonda Beach

Labelled as one of the most beautiful beaches in Asia, Agonda beach truly lives to this title. The beach is serene and clean owing to the fact that street vendors and hawkers are banned from the area. It is an ideal beach to spend time relaxing and meditating.


Candolim Beach

Located 15 Kms from the Capital City of Panjim, Candolim beach is a peaceful beach which is located quiet close to the Aguada beach. It is preferred over the other beaches owing to its serene and tranquil environment. Candolim beach also offers various sports and has some of the finest seafood shacks along it.


Vagator Beach

Vagator beach is divided into two, Vagator and Ozran. Ozran beach is a small rocky beach, with coconut trees along the coast, it’s a great place to seek solitude as you are engulfed in nature, and is a nice alternative to other busier beaches. The main beach is livelier and more commercial.


Anjuna Beach

Infamous for the hippies that inhabited the area, Anjuna Beach is a vibrant and lively beach in North Goa. Although small in size the beach has inviting blue waters and beach beds for those who just want to bask in the sun. The belt has a number of famous party shacks that offer breathtaking sea views.


Cavellosim Beach

Situated in the South of Goa, Cavellosim Beach is famous for its Virgin white sands that are a contrast to the deep brown rocks. Cavellosim Beach offers tourists a lot of activities like dolphin cruises, water sports and other activities.


Morjim Beach

Situated in North Goa, Morjim is a tranquil beach and counted among the most beautiful beaches in Asia. Serene and scenic, Morjim has some of the best beach side shacks and lounges. Amongst the many unique things offered is a chance to admire the views of Chapora Fort on the opposite side, and perhaps get a glimpse of the Olive Ridley turtle who nest on these shores.


Palolem Beach

Palolem is a popular beach in South Goa. It’s deep blue waters, golden sands and beautiful panoramic sunset views make this beach a visual treat. Along the beach you will find wooden huts and tempting beach side shacks. For the adventurous, there is Kayaking and an array of other water sports.


Colva Beach

This expansive beach in South has white sands and has a shore covered with coconut trees. Besides the regular commercial trappings of an idyllic Goan beach with the likes of water scooter, parasailing, boat rides, the beach is full of shells and star fish making your experience fulfilling and unique.

Nature

Springs and Waterfalls

Apart from its many beaches, Goa also has an abundance of lakes, springs and waterfalls. The sheer beauty of Dudhsagar and the tricky terrains of Hivre falls, are sure to take your breath away. Monsoons in Goa are really a visual treat, the rivers are in full swing, the springs decorate the valley with sparkling waterfalls, magnificent lakes, lush green trees and beautiful flowers. Some of the waterfalls worth mentioning would be Hirve, Dudhsagar, Tamdi surla, Netravali and Sada falls.


Spice Plantations

Goa has some of the oldest spice plantations in India. Guides available at the locations, walk you around the plantation, providing you with information on various spices and their medicinal values. These farms are mostly located in villages that are serene and untouched and strive to show you the perks of rural living in lush surroundings. A few things to look forward to at these farms are - elephant washing and riding, cultural dances and the very scrumptious cuisine.

Goa has nine beautiful rivers running through its veins. Experience a whole different side to the otherwise lively and buzzing Goa in these calm and peaceful backwaters. Take a boat ride, fish with the local fisherman, go bird-watching or simply relax along the banks of a river, there is no better way to escape from the humdrums of city life.


Wildlife

Goa is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. A large number of environmental enthusiasts and photographers come here to witness the rich biodiversity. The adventurous can go trekking or hiking into the wild. Goa has a number of wildlife sanctuaries such as Mhadei Wildlife Sanctuary, Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary and Netravali Wildlife Sanctuary.

Heritage

The Forts

Forts in Goa may not be as big in sizes as its Indian counterparts, but they are equally soaked in history and legacy. Forts like Reis Magos and Aguada are located at convenient distances from the beaches and resorts and offer breathtaking views of the landscape and the coastline. Other forts like Chapora, Terekol, Cabo de Ram, Marmugao and Rachol are worth exploring for the history enthusiast.


Churches & Temples

Most of the churches in Goa were built during Portuguese era. Churches in Goa are renowned for their interesting architecture which is a blend of two or more styles and has various architectural influences. Most of the temples are surrounded by large well maintained courtyards, mostly with an attached water body that serves as a ritualistic cleaning place to devotees. A drive from Panaji to Ponda via Old Goa will cover most of the major churches and temples in Goa.


Architecture

The Architecture in Goa is debatably the only remaining account of the wide spectrum of its rich heritage. The Architecture retains the functional elements of the pre-colonial period but also showcases the subsequent influence of European architecture with its elements of classical, gothic and manueline design forms.


The Furniture

A blend of functionality and art, the furniture in the old houses of Goa is soaked with heritage. Whether it’s a rocking chair or a well carved sofa, Goan furniture stands account of the European influence on the Goan heritage. The style of the Goan craftsmen is unique and antique which makes th furniture of great demand all over India. Contemporary designs include colorful tiled work on wood, making the furniture a piece of fusion art.

Water Sports

Parasailing

In this thrilling activity a person is attached to a parachute and is towed behind a speed boat. The boat circles the blue waters while you soar sky high and enjoy glorious coastal views. You will be equipped with safety gear and will be briefed before you take off the white sands.


Scuba-Diving

Witness the aquatic life with this underwater activity. Equipped with a wet suit and a breathing apparatus, Scuba-Diving makes it possible for you to grow gills and explore a whole new world, a new eco-system and much more as you scuba-dive into the exotic underwater spots by the beautiful sea.


Banana Boat

This fun and exciting water activity comprises of a yellow banana shaped tube which holds 6-8 people and is pulled by a speed-jetty while the passengers try to hold on and prevent a capsize which is the inevitable result and a splash of fun.


Jet Ski

If boats are the cars of the waters, the Jet Ski’s are the two-wheeler of the seas. Offered in most of the beaches of the State you will surely enjoy speeding through the waters with these mean machines.


Snorkelling

While Scuba diving will need you to immerse yourself completely into a certain depth of a water body, another simpler option to observe the sea or aquatic life can be through snorkelling. Equipped with a diving mask, a snorkel, fins and in some cases wet suits, this is an exhilarating experience of exploring or having a glimpse into another part of life which is normally out of sight for us.


Kayaking

Take part in this adventurous and unforgettable experience and explore the breathtaking beauty of Goa’s blue waters with your own Canoe or by Kayaking. Explore the rivers of Mandovi and Zuari and the backwaters of Sal which are ideal for this scenic and thrilling activity.


Sailing

Invest time learning the art of Sailing or ask to be accompanied by one of the friendly guides, the discretion is yours! Sailing holds endless possibilities for all, the thrill of steering your own boat and greeting friendly dolphins along the way is absolutely fulfilling.

locations

Reis Magos Fort

Four mighty forts once defended the river gate to the port town of Old Goa. Forte Aguada, Forte Real (now Reis Magos), Forte Gaspar Dias and Forte Cabo, all strategically placed on the mouth of the river Mandovi. Then a strategy, today remains of these forts provide some of the most breath taking views of the sea. The Reis Magos Fort has been restored & has a variety of art insertions by the Famous Goan cartoonist Mario Miranda. The fort plays host to exclusive events and functions.


Fort Aguada

Agua means water in Portuguese. Given to the fact that this fort had sweet water resource, the fort was named Aguada. Standing at the northernmost tip of the river mouth, this fort was always the first line of defense. Ships also used to dock here for supplies and water. The four storied light house at the fort is one of the oldest in Asia and most identifiable feature of the fort.


Old Goa

The best place to witness the footprint left by the Portuguese in Goa is in its old capital city of Old Goa. At the center is the magnificent Se Cathedral, a stunning cathedral built in the 17th century. The Basilica Bom Jesus is located just opposite, it is a world heritage site and apart from its unique baroque architecture, it is of great religious significance. There is an aura of tranquility surrounding Old Goa, and it is a great place to spend those much needed moments of Solace.


Fontainhas

Situated in the Capital city of Panjim, this charming 19th-century neighbourhood constitutes the largest and most significant Latin Quarters in Asia. Brightly coloured houses, inspirational architecture and quiet pathways contribute to the charm. A walk through the quaint streets reveals to you a whole different aspect of Goa, a Goa whose Portuguese heritage thrives more than 5 decades after Liberation.


Villages

The most charming parts of Goa, are undoubtedly its quaint villages with its lavish Portuguese houses, silent streets and friendly locals. Learn a little about rural living while you walk or ride through the Villages of Chandor or Aldona. A few houses even let visitors in to relive the old Indo- Portuguese lifestyle, the houses are massive and you will be left in awe as you walk under magnificent chandeliers and over intricately designed flooring.

Architecture

The Shell Window

In theory an influence of the Neo-Manueline Architecture, the shell window is one of the key elements defining the Indo-Portuguese Architectural heritage of Goa.


Vibrant Colours

Here is yet another story that explains the vibrant coloured houses. During the colonial times, churches and chapels which sprung up in and around the settlements were painted white as a symbol of holiness and purity. To make them stand out distinctly from the rest of the buildings, the Portuguese insisted on painting them with bright and vibrant colours.


High Ceilings

A feature of the Neo-Gothic design, high ceilings with huge open spaces is predominantly seen in the churches built by the Portuguese. This design has also seeped into the architecture of the mansion houses built during the early 18th century by the wealthy Goan families.


The Balcao

The Shady front pillared porches is another key element of the Indo-Portuguese Architecture. Although the design roots can be traced back to pre-Portuguese times, the The emphasis upon the decorative style and the ornate is surely an influence of the neo-classic and gothic designs. An incorporation of the same is also seen in the design of the wrought iron balconies hanging on the heritage houses.


The Furniture

A blend of functionality and art, the furniture in the old houses of Goa is soaked with heritage. Whether it is the rocking chair or the well carved sofa, these furniture stand account of the western influence on the Goan heritage.
